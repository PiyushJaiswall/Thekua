Production Process (7 Steps)

1
Water Lily Cultivation: Seeds germinate in ponds (Bihar, Darbhanga, Madhubani regions)
2
Seed Collection: Harvested from pond bottom by skilled workers (Mallah community)
3
Cleaning & Drying: Seeds cleaned of mud, shells. Sun-dried to reduce 20% moisture
4
First Roasting: High-temperature roasting to make seed coats brittle
5
Popping: Seeds popped to reveal white kernel inside
6
Polishing & Grading: Sorted by size (Sutta grades 3-6), polished for appearance
7
Final Roasting: Rotary roaster for crunchiness and flavor addition

üìå Strategic Approach

This roadmap outlines a systematic, phase-wise approach to launching and scaling your traditional snacks business from concept to market leader.

Phase 1: Foundation & Planning (Months 1-3)

Market Research & Business Setup

  • Legal Registration: Register company (Pvt Ltd/LLP), obtain PAN, TAN
  • FSSAI Registration: Apply for Basic/State/Central license based on turnover target
    • Turnover < ‚Çπ12 lakhs: Basic Registration (‚Çπ2,211 for 2 years)
    • ‚Çπ12 lakhs - ‚Çπ20 crores: State License (‚Çπ6,991 annual)
    • > ‚Çπ20 crores or multi-state: Central License (‚Çπ18,600 annual)
  • GST Registration: Mandatory if turnover exceeds ‚Çπ20 lakhs (‚Çπ10 lakhs for special states)
  • Trade License: From local municipal corporation
  • Fire Safety NOC: For manufacturing facility
  • Pollution Control Certificate: From state pollution board
  • Market Analysis: Finalize target cities, customer segments, pricing strategy
  • Supplier Identification: Source raw materials from Bihar (makhana, sattu), local suppliers
  • Competitor Analysis: Study pricing, positioning, gaps in market
  • Product Development: Recipe standardization, taste testing, shelf-life testing

Phase 2: Infrastructure Setup (Months 3-6)

Manufacturing & Technology Infrastructure

  • Facility Selection:
    • Option A: Own manufacturing unit (‚Çπ50 lakhs - ‚Çπ2 crores investment)
    • Option B: Contract manufacturing/Co-packing (‚Çπ10-30 lakhs initial)
    • Recommended: Start with co-packing, scale to own facility
  • Machinery Procurement:
    • Makhana: Roasting machine, grading machine, flavoring drum
    • Thekua: Dough mixer, molding equipment, fryer/oven
    • Sattu: Roaster (20kg/hour), grinder (25kg/hour), sieving machine
    • Mathri/Nimki: Dough sheeter, cutter, fryer, cooling conveyor
    • Packaging: VFFS machine, nitrogen flushing, weighing scales, sealing machine
  • Technology Implementation:
    • Batch Tracking System: Unique codes for each production batch
    • Inventory Management: ERP system for raw materials, finished goods
    • Quality Control Software: Digital logs, temperature monitoring
    • E-commerce Platform: Website, Amazon, Flipkart listings
    • CRM System: Customer data, order management
  • Quality Systems:
    • HACCP implementation: Hazard analysis, critical control points
    • SOP documentation: 20+ SOPs for production, cleaning, safety
    • Testing lab setup: Basic equipment or tie-up with external lab

Phase 3: Product Development & Packaging (Months 5-7)

Product Launch Preparation

  • Recipe Finalization:
    • Makhana: Plain roasted, Peri peri, Pudina, Salt & pepper (4 SKUs)
    • Thekua: Traditional, Jaggery, Coconut (3 SKUs)
    • Sattu: Powder 500g, Sattu bars (2 SKUs)
    • Mathri/Nimki: Ajwain, Plain, Methi (3 SKUs)
  • Packaging Design:
    • Hire packaging design agency (‚Çπ40,000 - ‚Çπ2 lakhs)
    • Create brand identity: Logo, colors, typography
    • Pack sizes: 50g, 100g, 250g, 500g, 900g
    • Material: Food-grade BOPP, metallized film with nitrogen flushing
    • Labeling: Comply with Legal Metrology, FSSAI, nutritional information
  • Nutritional Testing: Get lab analysis for nutrition facts panel
  • Shelf-Life Testing: Conduct accelerated and real-time studies
  • Prototype Production: Small batch production (100-500 units) for testing
  • Focus Group Testing: Gather feedback from 50-100 potential customers

Phase 4: Pre-Launch & Pilot (Months 7-9)

Market Testing & Refinement

  • Pilot Production: 10,000 units per product (40,000 total)
  • Distribution Pilot:
    • Launch in 1-2 cities (e.g., Delhi-NCR, Mumbai)
    • Secure 20-30 retail stores (kirana, modern trade)
    • Launch on 2-3 e-commerce platforms
  • Marketing Campaigns:
    • Social media setup: Instagram, Facebook, YouTube
    • Influencer collaborations: 10-15 micro-influencers
    • Content creation: Product videos, recipes, health benefits
    • Google Ads & Facebook Ads: ‚Çπ50,000-1 lakh budget
  • Feedback Collection: Surveys, reviews, direct customer interactions
  • Iteration: Refine taste, packaging, pricing based on feedback
  • Operations Testing: Validate supply chain, inventory, delivery timelines

Pricing Strategy Framework

1. Cost-Plus Pricing (Foundation)

  • Calculate full cost: Raw material + Labor + Overhead + Packaging
  • Add desired margin: 40-60% gross margin target
  • Example: If total cost = ‚Çπ200/kg, target selling price = ‚Çπ280-320/kg (wholesale)

2. Competitive Pricing (Market Positioning)

  • Premium Strategy: Price 15-25% above market leader if quality/uniqueness justifies
    • Example: If Mr. Makhana sells at ‚Çπ600/kg, position at ‚Çπ690-750/kg with superior ingredients
  • Parity Strategy: Match competitor pricing, differentiate on other factors
    • Example: Match Farmley's pricing but offer better packaging or more variants
  • Penetration Strategy: Price 10-20% below competitors to gain initial market share
    • Risk: Lower margins initially, brand perception challenges

3. Value-Based Pricing (Customer Perception)

  • Assess what customers willing to pay based on:
    • Health benefits (organic, preservative-free, natural)
    • Convenience (ready-to-eat, portion control packs)
    • Brand story (authentic Bihar recipe, farmer partnerships)
    • Taste & quality superiority
  • Example: Organic makhana can command 30-40% premium over regular

4. Channel-Specific Pricing

Quality Assurance & Food Safety

HACCP Implementation (Hazard Analysis & Critical Control Points)

Mandatory for export and recommended for all food businesses

7 Principles:
1
Conduct Hazard Analysis: Identify biological, chemical, physical hazards (e.g., contamination, allergens, foreign objects)
2
Determine Critical Control Points (CCPs): E.g., frying temperature, roasting time, metal detection
3
Establish Critical Limits: E.g., frying temp 170-180¬∞C, moisture < 3%
4
Establish Monitoring Procedures: Hourly temp checks, visual inspections, automated sensors
5
Establish Corrective Actions: If temp drops, reheat batch; if foreign object detected, reject entire batch
6
Establish Verification Procedures: Weekly audits, third-party lab testing monthly
7
Establish Record-Keeping: Digital logs of all checks, test results, corrective actions (batch tracking)
